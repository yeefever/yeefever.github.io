The modern roguelike category of video games covers quite a wide family but the core premise that **death is not permanent** is consistent. The games are designed with enough randomness (really? or so I hypothesize) to make each attempt (or as they are called coloquially "run") feel meaningfully different. The player feels rewarded for failing, dying, losing. Not only are they rewarded with direct learning about the game but also often times meta-currency to improve their chances of success in the next run.
